Writing Tips

Everyone needs some tips every now and then. Here are a few tips from grammar to getting out of writers block as well as improve your writing skills.

Set Writing Goals

Setting up this goal can help you reach a set number of pages or words. It can help extend your chapters and provide more plot in them as well. But be careful, this can be a danegerous goal as sometimes you want to reach the goal at any cost.

Time of Day to Write

For some people writing in the morning have an easy writing flow, not to mention cause less gramatic errors in their writing according to Grammarly research. However maybe you found you write better in the afternoon or when it becomes night. Choose a time of day that best fits for you.

Inspiration

One of the big negative elements that his authors is lack of inspiration. Gainging it back can be extrememly difficult and troublesome. However, it can also hit you in the oddest places. One of these can be research or even watching a random youtube video or animation. Inspiration can come from anywhere form a child chasing a dog to an anime you just started watching. So get out there and observe.

Outline

Outlining your story can greatly improve your writing. Having a plan how you want the story to come out can help keep you on track so you aren't going everywhere. You can create a powerpoint or even write it down in a notebook.

Be a Story Teller

Humans have been drawn to stories ever since ancient times. Your story doesn't have to be exactly resonable or if it can't happen in real life. However you can't go too above and beyond, or really too dramatic with the story. Keep is reasonable but at the same time have fun with it.

Avoid cliche

Look for overused adjective words along with overused phrases in your story. Change them to something more unique that you haven't written down a few times. Also avoid common actions in certain genre types. A big one is romance, come up with something new and not the usual you would find.

Keep a List of Often Mistakes

Find what your negative attributes in writing, or really your writing mistakes. If you write down the mistakes you commonly make you will most likely find them much easier.

Use Sensory

Using your five senses as you write and describe things in your writing can be very helpful for your readers as they picture what you are describing. However you don't have to describe everything by detail. For example, don't describe a toaster just say toaster along with its color if you want to. Unless your character doesn't know what a toaster is.

Become Your Character

As you write, place yourself in your character's shoes, or even imagine your standing where they are. This can help with sensory and description of what is going on around your character(s).

These are some of the most important tips, but not all of them. Visit Grammarly or SmartBlogger for some futher tips!
